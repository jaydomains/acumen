## OV-P1 — Merge-class self-classification: the detail plan is **class (iv)**, must NOT auto-merge

A governance-correct detail plan must **self-classify its own merge as class (iv)
ratification-class** and state that it does **not** auto-merge on the three-sign-off gate. The
detail plan bakes the **slice-level decisions, the anchor numbering surface (AC-D32/D33,
AC-CD27/28), the intra/inter-phase sequencing, and the launch-blocker classification** — these are
a **scope decision (iii)** and set **durable project-level precedent (iv)** that binds every
downstream phase/slice PR. Per role files §8.3 and `REQUIRED_READING.md` §7, that requires
**explicit authenticated spec-author ratification before the overseer executes**.

*Watch-for (the load-bearing merge-executor failure, overseer §8.3):* the draft mis-classifying its
merge **down** to NORMAL / "plans/** only, auto-merges on three sign-offs." A `plans/**`-only diff
does **not** make it NORMAL — the parent (#130) was `plans/**`-only and was correctly class (iv).
If the draft asserts auto-merge, that is an `OV` Push-back, and **I hold the merge regardless of
what the draft asserts** — classification is mine to enforce, not the draft's to declare away.

## OV-P2 — Channel discipline: the opener/relayed "settled direction" does not authorize my merge

The detail plan must not treat the session-opener rulings (v1.10, DP-4 gated, DP-9 deferred, DP-10
hybrid, leans confirmed) — however they reach the loop — as **self-authorizing merge**. Every
role-session writes under the **same shared identity**, so the planner's (or my opener's) recording
of an in-session ratification is, to me as gate-keeper, a **relay** indistinguishable from origin
by authorship (role files §8.3; the PR #130 OV-2 call). A governance-correct draft states
explicitly that the class-(iv) ratification authorizing merge must **affirm the detail plan's
decision surface itself** (the resolved DPs + anchor numbering + sequencing + launch-blocker set)
through the **authenticated direct channel** at the convergence SHA — not merely cite the opener.

*Watch-for:* a draft loop-mechanics section that records "ratified in-session, auto-merges on three
sign-offs," or that folds the merge-authorization into the relayed leans. I **never merge on a
relayed/recorded/inferred/superseded ruling**; I confirm through the authenticated channel and act
only on the **current** ruling.
